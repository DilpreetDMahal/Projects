  • Chicken Rice

    1. For this meal, you will need chicken, rice, chicken broth, oil, onion, garlic and seasonings.
    2. Toss the chicken with the spices. Heat the oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the chicken for 4 minutes per side until browned, then remove it and set aside.
    3. In the same pan, add the onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until soft. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
    4. Add the rinsed rice to the pan and stir for 1-2 minutes to toast it slightly, scraping up any delicious browned bits from the chicken.
    5. Pour in the chicken broth and stir. Nestle the browned chicken back into the rice, bring to a gentle boil, then cover with a tight-fitting lid. Reduce the heat to low and let it steam undisturbed for 20 minutes. Remove from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes before serving!

  • Rice and Curry

    1. Heat 1–2 tablespoons of oil or ghee in a large pot over medium heat. Add chopped onions and cook until translucent. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, stirring constantly for about 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
    2. Add your spices (such as curry powder, turmeric, and cumin) and stir for 15–30 seconds to toast them. Add your diced protein (chicken, beef, or vegetables) and stir until completely coated in the spiced oil and browned on the outside.
    3. Pour in your liquids, which can be a combination of chicken or vegetable broth, coconut milk, or tomato paste. Bring to a gentle simmer, then turn the heat to medium-low. Cover and let the curry cook until the meat is tender and the sauce is thick (usually 15–25 minutes).
    4. While the curry simmers, rinse your rice (like basmati or jasmine) under cold water until it runs clear. Cook the rice separately using your preferred method (a typical ratio is 1 part rice to 2 parts water). Serve the freshly cooked rice alongside or underneath the rich curry sauce.
